Case Study

For my project, I will design a webpage for a modern IT security company, BAI Security. The content for this website was provided by 99designs.com. The company specializes in cost-effective security solutions for industries such as healthcare, the financial sector, education, and government. This is the following content for the website provided by the client: - primary and secondary colors, a logo, and images from Adobe Stock for the website. The website will be used to allow the user to view the company's services, client testimonials, winning awards, action buttons in multiple locations, and contact information. I will create a different layout for the website, and I will try to avoid using too many images, heavy text, and cartoon illustrations. I will try to use more modern colors and fonts. The client provides the persona and a target audience for the website, so I will focus on that to support the client's idea and target audience.

The resources I will be using are the images from Adobe Stock and the icons from font awesome. Also, the font I will use is from Google Fonts. Resizing and cropping the images will be done in Adobe Photoshop. The color I will use that provided by the client, but I will modify them to fit the design through colorhunt.co. Any additional photos I will gather through www.pexels.com. The prototype I will design in Adobe XD. The logo was provided by the client and if I need to resize it, I will use Adobe Photoshop. The code will be written in Visual Studio Code.

Target Audience:

  • Financial
  • Medical
  • Insurance
  • Utilities
  • Legal

Persona:

These are mostly executive-level folks with tech knowledge, but they're not by any means the super-techies that work under them. They are more compliance-focused and have an enterprise-wide lens on things. In decision-making, they're likely to attend to detail, so “meat” on the website has to validate that we're the real deal. See note under style re: how we could offer this content without muddying pages as much as they currently are.

About 2/3rds are male, and a majority are white and in the financial sector (per note about the history of our clientele, but focusing on healthcare heavily to balance going forward).
